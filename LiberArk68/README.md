# LiberArk68 — Case Files

3D-printable case for the LiberArk68 split keyboard. Files are organized by
switch type, with optional variants for the add-on plate, low-profile battery
mounting, and slide faders.

## Printing a complete case

Each **half** needs three parts — a **Bottom**, a **Top**, and a **Plate**.
A full keyboard is two halves.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The provided files are for the **left** half. **Mirror** the parts in your
> slicer to produce the **right** half.

## Switch type

Pick the folder that matches your switches:

- **`MX/`** — MX-style switches
- **`Choc/`** — Kailh Choc (low-profile) switches

## Part variants

| Part / suffix | Folder | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Bottom / Top / Plate** | both | Standard build |
| **`-Addon` Bottom & Top** | both | Using the add-on plate for roller encoders or touchpads — print **both** the `-Addon` bottom and top |
| **`-LowProfile` Bottom** | both | Shaves ~2 mm off total height with a battery cutout in the bottom (see warning) |
| **`-FlatTop` Top** | Choc | Easier, support-free printing — orients the top face on the build plate |
| **Fader Top** | both | For 75 mm / 60 mm-travel slide faders (see below) |

> [!WARNING]
> The **`-LowProfile`** bottoms leave the battery **exposed** on the underside.
> Not recommended if the keyboard will travel.

> [!NOTE]
> The Choc **`-FlatTop`** top is purely a print-orientation convenience — same
> fit as the standard top.

## Faders

The Fader tops fit **75 mm length / 60 mm travel** slide faders.

- Recommended: [Mouser 652-PTA60432015DPB10](https://www.mouser.com/ProductDetail/652-PTA60432015DPB10)

## Battery mounting

> [!TIP]
> Attach the battery to the PCB with **low-profile double-sided tape**, and use
> the **battery cutout in the bottom case** as a placement guide.
